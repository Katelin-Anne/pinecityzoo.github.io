KOALA

The koala (Phascolarctos cinereus) is a tree-dwelling marsupial native to Australia. Recognized by its round, fluffy ears, spoon-shaped nose, and thick grey fur, the koala is often mistaken for a bear but is in fact a marsupial, meaning females carry their young in a pouch.

Koalas feed almost exclusively on eucalyptus leaves, which provide them with both food and most of their water intake. Because eucalyptus leaves are low in nutrients, koalas sleep up to 18â€“20 hours a day to conserve energy. Their strong limbs and sharp claws help them climb and grip tree branches securely.

They are solitary animals and spend most of their time in trees, coming down only to move between feeding areas. Koalas communicate through a range of vocalizations, including deep bellows, especially during mating season.
