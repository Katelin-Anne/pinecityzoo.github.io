WARTHOG

The warthog (Phacochoerus africanus) is a wild member of the pig family found in savannahs, grasslands, and open woodlands across sub-Saharan Africa. Warthogs are easily recognized by their large curved tusks, facial warts, and sparse bristly hair on their bodies. Despite their somewhat fierce appearance, they are primarily grazers and browsers.

They feed mainly on grasses, roots, berries, and bark, but will also dig for bulbs and tubers using their strong snouts and tusks. Warthogs can survive in dry habitats by feeding on underground plant parts and often kneel on their front legs while grazing.

Warthogs live in groups called sounders, typically made up of females and their young, while males are more solitary. When threatened, they can run quickly, often with their tails held upright like a flag. They use abandoned burrows of aardvarks for shelter and protection against predators.
